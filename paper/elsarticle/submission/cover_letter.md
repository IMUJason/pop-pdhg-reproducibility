# Cover Letter

March 9, 2026

Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, **“A Hybrid Population PDHG Heuristic for Feasibility Recovery in Mixed-Integer Programming,”** for publication as a Research Article in *Expert Systems with Applications*.

This manuscript addresses a specific but practically important gap in intelligent optimization for mixed-integer programming: many relaxation-centered heuristics can produce strong fractional trajectories, yet still fail to cross the final barrier to strict feasibility. To address this issue, the paper proposes a hybrid population primal-dual hybrid gradient heuristic (HP-PDHG) that combines population-based PDHG search with barrier-aware non-local perturbation, progressive integrality projection, and conservative feasibility repair. The contribution is therefore not a claim about quantum hardware advantage, but an interpretable intelligent-search architecture for feasibility recovery in discrete optimization.

The manuscript makes four main contributions to the current literature. First, it introduces a hybrid feasibility-recovery heuristic that integrates first-order optimization with population search and barrier-sensitive exploration. Second, it rebuilds the empirical pipeline around audited MPS parsing, original constraint senses, explicit post-repair feasibility verification, and public benchmark references, thereby strengthening the methodological reliability of the reported results. Third, it demonstrates robust feasibility recovery on an audited four-instance benchmark and extends the evidence to an official MIPLIB-based study and a representative metaheuristic comparison against GA, DE, SHADE, and SL-PSO. Fourth, it complements the benchmark evidence with ablation and parameter-sensitivity analyses to clarify which components are decisive and which are supportive.

We believe the manuscript fits *Expert Systems with Applications* because it focuses on the design, analysis, and evaluation of an intelligent hybrid heuristic for a difficult real-world optimization setting. The paper emphasizes algorithmic structure, robustness, interpretability, and reproducibility rather than unsupported claims of universal superiority. In support of reproducibility, the code, manuscript source, experiment scripts, and archived processed results are available in the public repository: https://github.com/IMUJason/pop-pdhg-reproducibility. Official benchmark instances are retrieved from the public MIPLIB archives rather than redistributed in the repository.
